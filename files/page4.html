About Taylor

As many people know, Taylor Swift is one of the most popular female artists in our generation. We all know about her albums and songs, but what of her personal life?

Fun Fact: Taylor Swift was named after singer-songwriter James Taylor.

Taylor Alison Swift was born on December 13, 1989 in West Reading, Pennsylvania. She was born to Scott Kingsley Swift, her father, and Andrea Gardner Swift, her mother. Her younger brother, Austin Scott Swift, is an actor and producer. He currently manages Taylor's film projects and music licensing. When she was 9, Taylor became interested in musical theather and performed in four Berks Youth Theatre Academy productions. Taylor also regularly traveled to New York City for vocal and acting lessons. She also spent weekends performing at local festivals and events. She later shifted her focus toward country music.

After watching a documentary, Taylor felt sure that she should to move to Nashville, Tennessee to pursue to a career in music. When she was 11 she visited Nashville record labels to submit demo tapes of karaoke covers, but was rejected. At an age of 12 years old, she was taught how to play the guitar by computer repairman and local musician Ronnie Cremer. ... When she was 14, her father transferred to a Nashville branch of his office, and the family relocated to Hendersonville, Tennessee.

In 2004 she signed a songwriting deal with Sony/ATV Music Publishing and in 2005 she signed a recording contract with Big Machine Records. She released her self-titled debut album in 2006, making her the first female country singer to write or co-write a US a platinum certified album entirely.

Last May 18, 2022, Taylor Swift received a Doctor of Fine Arts, honoris causa, at the Commencement for the Class of 2022, and delivered remarks on behalf of the honorary degree recipients.
