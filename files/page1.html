Discography

Since 2006, Taylor Swift has been releasing hit after hit. She has released 10 studio albums- 2 of which have been re-recorded- 4 extended plays, 3 live albums, and others.


Taylor Swift (2006)


Fearless (2008/2021)

Speak Now (2010)


Red (2012/2022)

1989 (2014)

Reputation (2017)

Lover (2019)


Folklore (2020)

Evermore (2020)


Midnights (2022)

Taylor Swift is the self-titled debut album of Taylor Swift. It was recorded in 2005 and was released on October 24, 2006 by Big Machine Records. Taylor wrote/co-wrote 11 of the album's tracks, which were about her outlook on life as a teenager, dealing with romantic relationships, friendships, and insecurity. Taylor Swift is a country record with pop and pop rock elements, incorporating acoustic instruments such as guitars, banjos, and fiddles.
Source: Wikipedia
Fearless is the second album by Taylor Swift. It was recorded in 2008 and was released on November 11, 2008 by Big Machine Records. It was written largely by Taylor, with seven of the thirteen written by herself. She also co-produced all the songs with Nathan Chapman, making this her debut as a record producer. Fearless is a country pop album. It mixes electronic guitars with classic country instruments like banjos, fiddles, mandolins, and acoustic guitars. On April 9, 2021, Fearless (Taylor's Version) was released, making it the first re-recorded album by Taylor Swift. The album consists of the re-recorded versions of the 19 tracks from the original, the re-recording of Taylor's 2010 single "Today Was a Fairytale", and six "From The Vault" tracks which were withheld from being released in the original version.
Sources: Wikipedia, Wikipedia
Released on October 25, 2010 through Big Machine Records, Speak Now is the third studio album by Taylor Swift. Taylor wrote it during the 2 years she was promoting her second studio album, Fearless. Speak Now is about her transition from adolescence into adulthood, with her confessions regarding love and heartache, plus some songs about her public image. This album was produced by Taylor and Nathan Chapman. It has country, pop, pop rock, and power pop genres. It also featured acoustic instruments, dynamic electric guitars, strings, and drums.
Sources: Wikipedia
Red, released on October 22, 2012 by Big Machine Records, is the fourth album by Taylor Swift. The album's title refers to the intense and "red" emotions that Taylor has experienced during this time. The songs show the complex and conflicting feelings that come from fading romance. On this album, Taylor expanded beyond her traditional country pop sound into more pop, country, and rock songs with folk and electronic elements. On November 12, 2021, Taylor released a re-recorded version of this album through Republic Records, naming it "Red (Taylor's Version"). It includes the re-recorded versions of the 20 songs from the original, a 10-minute-long version of "All Too Well", her own recordings of "Better Man" and "Babe", and six "From The Vault" tracks.
Sources: Wikipedia, Wikipedia
1989 is the fifth studio album by Taylor Swift, released on October 27, 2014 by Big Machine Records. Just like in her other albums, Taylor writes the songs based on her personal life, showing lighthearted perspectives on failed relationships compared to her previous hostile attitude. She was inspired by 1980s synth-pop, with heavy synthesizers, programmed drums, and processed backing vocals.
Sources: Wikipedia
Repuataion, which was released on November 10, 2017 by Big Machine Records, is Taylor's sixth studio album. She wrote this album after media outlets highly publicized her personal life and disputes with other celebrities. Taylor wrote the album with two major themes: the excessive gossip surrounding Taylor and finding love during all this. The album is built around electropop, R&B, and trap, inspired by genres like EDM, hiphop, and Miami bass.
Sources: Wikipedia
Released on August 23, 2019 through Republic Records, Lover is the seventh studio album by Taylor. This album was her first album release after her departure from Big Machine Records. Taylor wrote Lover as a "love letter to love", drawing inspiration from her personal life and newfound artistic freedom. Lover has a brighter sound, with electropop, pop rock, and synth-pop.
Sources: Wikipedia
Folklore is the eight studio album by Taylor. It was a surprise release on July 24, 2020, via Republic Records. Taylor wrote Folklore as "a collection of songs and stories that flowed like a stream of consciousness". It explores the themes of escapism, empathy, romance, and nostalgia. Taylor tells the story through a set of characters and fictional stories compared to her previous autobiographical style. This album consists of mellow ballads with neo-classical instruments, indie folk, alternative rock, and electroacoustic styles.
Sources: Wikipedia
Released on December 11, 2020 by Rebuplic Records, Evermore is the ninth studio album by Taylor. Taylor described Evermore as an offshoot of "the folklorian woods", an escapist, cottagecore-inspired, alternative music style. It is about love, marriage, infidelity, and grief. It explores the complexities of human emotion. Evermore blends alternative rock, indie folk, and chamber pop styles with fingerpicked guitars, somber pianos, lavish strings, and sparse percussion.
Sources: Wikipedia
Midnights, released on October 21, 2022 through Republic Records, is the tenth studio album by Taylor. She announced this album at the 2022 MTV Video Music Awards. Midnights is about nocturnal contemplation. Taylor described the album as a "journey through terrors and sweet dreams". It was written and produced by Taylor with the collaboration of Jack Antonoff. For this album, Taylor used electronica, synth-pop, and chill-out music styles.
Sources: Wikipedia
